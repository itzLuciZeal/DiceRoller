Welcome To My New Random Project!

This journey took me a whole probably around 9 hours up to this point with just building the sketelon of this page. I have not yet been taking a break but this one is the best project I have done so far. I can lead you to my journey on what I went through if you have time to read it.

You might think, what project did I do? Well, it's simple really, a dice rolling program. The story was, I was just following Bro Code's Full Course on JavaScript 󰌞. And when I was just doing well, following the basics and stuff, I got into the dice rolling program project part. Guess what, I did this website for fun and curiosity!

What Bro Code did was a simple dice program that is 2D pictures and stuff where we learn applications of the spread operator and the rest operator. I was like, "This is going to be a breezed for me, what if I just did 3D dice cube instead?" then it hit me. It's difficult, but that's the thing, it was fun for me.

I was experimenting, tried using Gemini AI to help me with this one but yeah I just folded it on my own. Here you can see at the image at the top, I tried disassembling it and tried to get out of it.

Here you can see that I am getting pretty much out of it. This is where the transform of CSS  comes into play. What I did was tried to juggle it out, looking 3D always to check if I did it right.

You can really see here that I keep looking whether I got it right or not, and made each faces to have colors on them so I can distinguished it. I did rotateX('angle') and this one which controls the x-axis basically the horizontal part and rotateY('angle') that controls the y-axis part which in sideways, vertically yes, and lastly the rotateZ('angle') controlling part of z-axis that moves clockwise or counterclockwise, they are essential so I can make it like a cube.

Here, I demonstrated the usage of CSS Properties, like the rotateX(90deg) part of stuff in the transform style property. The process was frustrating in the part of doing the faces of the cube, since I am learning the 3D space of this one.

Another feature I added is that you can change the surface of the cube whether in anime or not, you can visit it in the settings  page!

One thing to notice is that the theme has changed! Which is great to be honest. You can also customize your theme inside the settings  which is at the header of this page. Additionally, you can also edit your accent color there.
